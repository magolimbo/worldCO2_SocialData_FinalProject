Introduction

For a while now, we’ve been hearing about CO2 emissions, their dangers for the future of our planet, and for us who inhabit it. Every day, a TV in the background, a radio, or a voice on the street warns us of the dangers we’re facing, of the inevitable death spiral we’ve entered, of the irreversibility of our actions on the environment. It’s become such a daily white noise that it almost doesn’t scare us anymore, its effects not yet tangibly catastrophic enough to despair our concern.
Some are trying to change course and mitigate the damage.
Others continue undisturbed to operate as they always have, testing the planet’s patience.

But where are we really?

Are we truly plunging into the abyss?

What are the direct factors that most influence CO2 emissions, and what are the socioeconomic factors that indirectly influence this problem?

With this research, we aimed to answer these questions by passionately delving into the global evolution of CO2 emissions over space and time, to understand where we started from and the current situation. The research then continues in the subsequent chapters with an analysis of the factors directly influencing CO2 emissions and proceeds with a chapter dedicated to exploring external factors that indirectly affect this issue, such as global governance forms, the level of education of the population, etc., with the aim of uncovering new patterns and correlations.

For the curious ones, the primary dataset used for the analysis is provided by Our World In Data, offering a vast collection of data from 1900 to the present day. Each entry in the dataset represents a country in a specific year, with information regarding population, fuels usage, CO2 emissions per capita, contribution to the global warming and many more. In addition to this extensive dataset, we relied on external sources to gather global socioeconomic data and intersect this information with that of the initial dataset, in order to obtain a more comprehensive overview capable of meeting our objectives.

With the hope that this analysis opens your mind and leaves you with something to ponder, as it has done for us,
we wish you a good read.
