Introduction

For a while now, we’ve been hearing about CO2 emissions, their dangers for the future of our planet, and for us who inhabit it. Every day, a TV in the background, a radio, or a voice on the street warns us of the dangers we’re facing, of the inevitable death spiral we’ve entered, of the irreversibility of our actions on the environment. It’s become such a daily white noise that it almost doesn’t scare us anymore, its effects not yet tangibly catastrophic enough to despair our concern.
Some are trying to change course and mitigate the damage.
Others continue undisturbed to operate as they always have, testing the planet’s patience.

But where are we really?

Are we truly plunging into the abyss?

What are the direct factors that most influence CO2 emissions, and what are the socioeconomic factors that indirectly influence this problem?

With this research, we’ve sought to answer these questions, pondering them passionately.
